Benefits for users and professionals

Whether in the office or in the hospital, patients benefit from more informative and productive medical consultations.

They remain in control of the situation and participate more in making decisions that affect them.

They understand the decisions made together much better and therefore support them more strongly.

Physicians experience more productive, effective consultations and ward rounds.

They feel an even higher level of appreciation from their patients.

Effectiveness, appropriateness, and economic efficiency of the measures taken increase.

Nursing professionals are able to provide even more support to patients by assisting them, if necessary, in using EverAsk.

They feel an even higher level of appreciation from patients.

Customer satisfaction of hospitals and HMO practices increases; this strengthens their competitiveness.

Joint decision-making is likely to increase the efficiency, appropriateness and economic efficiency of the services provided.

As a result, hospitals and HMO practices expand the room for negotiation with health insurers.

Health insurers benefit from greater health literacy among their customers.

They expand the room for negotiation with hospitals and HMO practices.

They benefit from a better partnership climate in the health sector.

The EverAsk Mission:
To strengthen the patients' ability to conduct conversations with physicians

1

Patients are not health care professionals

Many patients do not think about preparing their conversations with medical doctors. After the conversation, they often realize that they have forgotten to ask one or the other question or to bring in an important piece of information.

The EverAsk Mission:
To strengthen the patients' ability to conduct conversations with physicians

1

Patients are often passive during consultations or ward rounds

Numerous studies show that patients have less say during conversations with physicians and ask fewer questions than health care professionals. Once settled into this passive role, it is often difficult for them to resume an active position.

The EverAsk Mission:
To strengthen the patients' ability to conduct conversations with physicians

1

Important information is not taken into consideration

When treatment decisions need to be made, patients are often not able to express their own preferences and priorities. If these preferences were taken into account, the decisions made would likely be more appropriate.

EverAsk - a tool for patient engagement

EverAsk helps patients prepare in a structured way, put their knowledge to use and participate in decision-making. They become full partners in the process and have more effective conversations.
